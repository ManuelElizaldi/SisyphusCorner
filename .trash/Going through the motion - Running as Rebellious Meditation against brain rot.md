Running, what started as a way of working out and staying healthy has slowly become a masochistic form of meditation for me. If you have never ran before, let me walk you through what it feels to start. 

First, you will feel your muscles screaming at you to stop, then you feel like you are running out of breath and if you are a sane person, your brain will tell you to halt immediately. Of the many sports I have practiced throughout my life, running could be the hardest to start. For untrained muscles, this is a display of raw pain. 

Comparing this to weightlifting, another of my favorite forms of exercise, running does not offer any breaks from the exertion. When you hit the gym you do an exercise for 8 - 15 reps and you get time to catch your breath, then go again. 

Running is the complete opposite, you run until you are done, not when you are tired. Not to discredit weightlifting, there are plenty of ways to torture yourself like Bulgarian squats or burpees, but any runner that has experience on both, will tell you that there is a special feeling you get for holding *This needs work*

pain close and dear the pain of running.  

There is also an added element of difficulty to running. If you are anything like me, running on a treadmill is the last thing you would ever want to do. So you head outside to run. Then the weather plays an important role. Sometimes you will be forced to run in the scorching heat of summer, other times the cold wind will be your companion, but this just makes the experience more visceral, more real. 

But why submit oneself to this level of suck? Honestly? Running makes me feel good, not just physically but mentally. Going for a run creates the perfect environment to disconnect. I work as a developer and spend too much time with computers. Although I love what I do, I recognize that too much sitting can ruin my mind and my body. Having 2 monitors in front of me makes it easy to get distracted and perpetuate the doom scrolling. The constant stimulation is borderline abuse. Brain rot ensues. There is a graceful respite from the dull stimulation of scrolling. Running without headphones accompanied by your own thoughts and demons. This is the rebellion I practice to fight the algorithm. 

When I run in this fashion I feel the sun in my skin, I hear the wind move the trees and the birds sing. I observe people walking by and feel my shoes hammering the floor moving me forward. During my run I immerse myself in the experience and my mind involuntarily surfaces unnoticed ideas and worries. Running provides a silence which we have cast away through the constant bombardment of stimulation. We have lost boredom, not realizing that within silence we can find ourselves.
