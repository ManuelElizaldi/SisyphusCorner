The clock marks 18:00. I am done with work, brain a bit fried from building data pipelines, the vibrations of stress receding throughout my body. I might be mentally tired, but the muscles are ready to hustle.

I have a portable gym sitting at the back of my car, with two 1 pood (36.11 pounds) kettle bells shaped like the heads of chimps, a couple of bands, a jumping rope and my 100 pound sandbag. This is enough equipment for me to get in a solid workout, plus this is not too much stuff so setting it up at parks is fairly easy. I can also travel to visit my family out of town without missing a workout. 

Today I am taking the kettlebells and sandbag to the top of my apartment's parking lot to train with Austin's buildings stretching in the horizon as pillars of motivation with a burnt sky as stage.

I park at the far end, open the trunk and unload the sandbag and kettle bells. I grab the jumping rope and warm up with simple jumps for 5 to 10 minutes. The impact of my feet hitting the concrete primes my legs for the toll that's to come. 

Sand, resting motionless, not wanting to be picked, 100 pounds of sand entrapped in this bag. I stare at this mass of potential gains, hesitate to start my workout. Then remember the historic lifting stones of Scotland, how these rocks that have been resting in place waiting for someone to accept the *what type of challenge*. Rocks that turn children into men. 

I start my workout with farmer carries, grabbing the sandbag with my hands, digging my fingers into its fabric to get a solid hold.
