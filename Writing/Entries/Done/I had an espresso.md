I am trying out a writing challenge. I woke up for work, got my morning programs running and now I made myself an espresso. The goal of this challenge is to write a mini essay while I drink my espresso, small edits, no frills, rapid fire writing. 

What inspired this little quest, was The Sopranos, after I don't know how many years from its release I finally decided to watch this mafioso masterpiece. I clearly remember when my dad was watching this back in the day. I can't say precisely how old I was, but I remember he had to travel for work to some other place I can't recall. 

This was the first time he had to travel without us and also perhaps the first time we spent so much time apart. I remember my mom and I called him to the hotel room. As a kid my imagination ran rampant, I saw adults and thought they had crazy lives, far from my ordinary school and activities. 

So being the curious rascal that I am, I asked him what he was doing at the hotel room all by himself, he said, "I am watching The Sopranos". I am not sure why that stuck with me for so long, but now I am here learning about Waste Management Consulting, seeing how some tough Italian dudes handle business while drinking espresso. 

Yesterday while I was watching the show, it started to rain. One of those rains that yanks your attention for a couple of seconds, pleading for recognition. "ah, it is raining" I said to myself. Slowly the rain receded as my episode reached the end. The water stopped falling around 8:20 pm, for some reason the sun did not set. With residual rain coating the sky and the refusal of the sun as inspiration, a beautiful pink canvas was painted out in front of me.

I opened my balcony window to appreciate this gift and noticed my next door neighbor was doing the same. We exchanged a salute, then turned to the other side and realized there was a double rainbow. My neighbor and I got excited, like little kids, appreciating this majestic scene.

Then, after the sun left, I started thinking about superstition, a curse, an agenda or perhaps providence, unforeseen hidden forces. Arsenal Football club won the English Premier League after a 22 year drought. One would argue that something of this magnitude, not winning the tournament for so long, requires an even bigger explanation. But perhaps good things just take time, and sometimes there's no happy ending. 

I thought about my friend, who has been a fan of this club since 2002? 2006? I don't know but I know he is one of the most loyal dudes I know. I can tell a lot from someone based on the team they support. I feel a bit envious of him, wanting to be part of something for so long, finally tasting the laurels of victory. 

I wanted to end this piece saying I made another espresso, but my coffee machine broke. Providence does not want me caffeinated today.
