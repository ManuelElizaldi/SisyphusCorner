Running, what started as a way of working out and staying healthy has slowly become a masochistic form of meditation for me. If you have never ran before, let me walk you through what it feels to start. 

First, you will feel your muscles screaming at you to stop, then you feel like you are running out of breath and if you are a sane person, your brain will tell you to halt immediately. Of the many sports I have practiced throughout my life, running could be the hardest to start. For untrained muscles, this is a display of raw pain. 

Comparing this to weightlifting, another of my favorite forms of exercise, running does not offer any breaks from the exertion. When you hit the gym you do an exercise for 6 - 15 reps and you get time to catch your breath, then go again. 

Running is the complete opposite, you run until you are done, not when you are tired. Not to discredit weightlifting's pain inducing exercises, there are plenty of ways to torture yourself in the gym. For example there's Bulgarian squats or burpees. But any runner that has experience on both, will tell you that there is a special feeling you get for tolerating the inherit pain of running.

There is also an added element of difficulty to running. If you are anything like me, running on a treadmill is the last thing you would ever want to do. So you head outside to run. Then the weather plays an important role. Sometimes you will be forced to run in the scorching heat of summer, other times the cold wind will be your dear companion, but this just makes the experience more visceral, more real. 

But why submit oneself to this level of suck? Honestly? Running makes me feel good, not just physically but mentally. Going for a run creates the perfect environment to disconnect. I work as a developer and spend too much time with computers. Although I love what I do, I recognize that too much sitting can ruin my mind and my body. Having 2 monitors in front of me makes it easy to get distracted and perpetuate the doom scrolling. The constant stimulation is borderline abuse. Brain rot ensues. Thankfully, there is a graceful respite from the dull stimulation of scrolling. Running without headphones accompanied by your own thoughts and demons. This is the rebellion I practice to fight the algorithm. 

When I run in this fashion I feel the sun in my skin, I hear the wind move the trees and the birds sing. I observe people walking by and feel my shoes hammering the floor moving me forward. During my run I immerse myself in the experience and my mind involuntarily surfaces unnoticed ideas and worries. Running offers a silence which we have cast away through the constant bombardment of stimulation.

Some days I have observed a fog that shrouds me, I feel sense of despair or pessimism. This comes when I am going overboard with my social media use. The days when I am spending more time on my phone are the least creative, less optimistic, more anxiety inducing. I am certain that being constantly online slashes the connection between my body and my spirit. The noise around me has muted what is within. 

During my runs I've noticed that my muscles are not the only part of my body that's working. My spirit emerges to help me endure the suffering that comes with this sport. My resilience, similar to my muscles gets trained through this pain, hardships during runs trains me for the challenges of life. 

The time spent running has given me the chance to pray, this is something I am doing more often now. Before this, distractions, work and daily life didn't allow me to do so, but I have become more consistent. Those unnoticed worries and thoughts that I mentioned earlier? Here's where I deal with them. The fog of negative thoughts gets blown away.

After a run, especially if it was challenging I feel invincible. I show myself that I am capable of doing hard things. Also, the act of exercising and praying makes me feel weightless and my head is flooded with inspiration. Usually after a run I have a million ideas for my creative work. One example is this essay you are reading right now, the idea originated while I was running a 10 km. 

Running, more than exercise has been a salvation for me. It's been there to offer its (un)comfortable respite of life inevitable challenges. After facing a layoff that tested my spirit, I had more time to dedicate to my running, which then led me to compete in several races. One of these was an ultra marathon in Coahuila, Mexico. 

Going into the race I knew that I was going to run like never before. 50 kilometers composed of 8 mountains that demanded the upmost respect. I had my strategy for the race memorized, my glucose gels for fuel packed and I trusted my training. When you are running an ultra marathon the best thing you can do is not think about how much is left. If you do, you'll drain your mental energy. The goals is not to run the 50 km, rather its best to focus on each individual step. One step at a time, bringing you closer to the finish line. Moving forward is the only objective.

During this 9 hour long run I felt a whole spectrum of emotions. I was shaken down and start to wonder what the hell I was doing in the middle of Coahuila's mountainous forest. Then I was submerged into a sublime reverie where my spirit moved me forward, which reminded me of what I am capable. No layoff was going to stop me and I was going to finish that race.

I am grateful for being able to exercise this way and take care of the fighting spirit within me. I've made it my life's mission to take care of myself through running, other forms of exercise and writing these reflective pieces.
