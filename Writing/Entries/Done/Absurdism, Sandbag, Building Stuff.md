As I feel the warmth of the afternoon in my body, at the last *fix this*. I stare at my hands, a burning sensation from holding the hard fabric of the sandbag that is kicking my ass during this workout. I noticed the familiar ink stains that have been a common occurrence in my body ever since I started using fountain pens. 

When I grab the sandbag, the stains are oddly comforting but paradoxical sight to behold. These hands wrote an essay a few moments ago and now are carrying a hundred pound sandbag. I hinge, place the sandbag in my lap while in a squatting position, hug it tight to my body to then then explode violently from the hips to throw it overhead.

The workings of my mind are unknown to me, a tornado of ideas that sometimes is as serene as a pond in the high mountains. Then becomes an earthquake of activity that is hard to control. Spontaneous thundery rain that may bloom into projects, essays or just fade into unexistence.

I have come to notice that workouts are the perfect weather station to monitor new ideas. During this session I remembered how I had to put this bag together. I ordered it online and just got the empty bag - a part of me expected the thing to be already filled up, but alas. I had to go to home depot and buy the filling, before this I had no idea there were different types of sand, leveling sand, play sand, paver sand. The different options lead me to google what was best. Bought 2 sacks that equal to a hundred pounds of dry play sand. Another perplexing thought, dry sand. I thought all sand was dry. Filling it was simple, I only had to remove the protective velcro, open the zipper and fill it up. In these simple steps I had created my own self destruction device. 

The sandbag hits the concrete making a loud 'thump' that shakes the floor of the parking lot where I am training. The noise zaps me back to the moment. Some dust or sand flows upward from the impact. At that moment I felt pride knowing I had built this sandbag for myself, then as easy as it came, the feeling of pride was demolished by a personal struggle I have encountered throughout my career.

Working with computers has given me a fulfilling career, there have been ups and downs that I've rode to the best of my abilities. 


There has always been a nagging sensation of existencialism tied to my work. Every solution I build is composed of numbers on a screen. Bits and pixels are the only evidence I have from where I stand. Although the code I write does bring positive impact to the patients, I do not see it. I can't hold the solution with myh ink stained hands. 

As I write these words I am holding my journal, my pen. Pages filled with evidence of the work I have done with my writing. Comfort comes back again. Several pages stained with the *strokes* of my life. Experiences turned into ink filled pages.
