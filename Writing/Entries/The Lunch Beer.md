Just another no frills piece. I am writing from my counciousness. 

The day is Wednesday, June 10th, 2026. I wake up having barely slept from exited anxiousness. It was a sort of a happy anxiety. A feeling of knowing something will happen and I wanted it to happen already. I started my day by logging into work. Running my reports and then patiently waited for 11:00 am. 

I had scheduled an cloud engineer/solutions architect certification exam at 12:00 am after 8 months of diligent studying. 8 months, 243 days, around 5800 hours. Rep after rep of studying an hour to two hours a day. Everything aimed at a promise for better work. I am not sure what metric I am using for 'better', but definitely something more fulfilling.

Always evolving, for it is in my nature to look for the next step. Rest does not come easy to me. I feel like I am always on the go, not sure if I have been grasped by the wave of optimization gurus. But there is always a nagging sensation of the "whats next". It also could be excitement for living. As I write this and glance at my library, searching for words, I realize I have the evidence of this behavior. Every time I look at book reviews on instagram reels, my want to read list grows and grows like roots taking hold of my brain. I checkout my list, and think to myself, "so many books and so little life." There is so much I want to read, so much I want to do. Perhaps that thirst for experience is the cause of my unrest. 

Regardless, I am sitting at my desk waiting for the time I can start heading out to Austin Community College testing center. Due to excitement, responsibility or nerves I arrive an hour early. I take this time to relax. I once read in a book called "learning how to learn" that taking a pause from studying allows the brain to trigger something called, diffuse brain. This is when the mind works in the background to plant all the seeds of knowledge. Connections between knowledge are established. Basically, this is when all the hard work pays off and your brain locks in the new studied material. 

I walked through campus, surprised at how many different characters I spotted. Some carried swagger with them, giving an impression of not being worried. There were others who I could plainly see that worked for the school. Some other dudes where just sitting down, focused on their assignments in their laptops. 

Each with its own worries, demands to the universe, reasons to live and - no reasons -. The wave of sonder that I was experiencing made me forget about my important exam for a bit. It put me in a state of relaxation. A calm before the storm type of deal. 

As I continued my pondering walk, I stumbled upon many pictures taken by students hanged throughout the building I was in. The pictures in the walls resembled the variety of people I could see. Each its own genre of photography. There were some modern matcha drinking cowboys, pictures of old parlors embodying the spirit of "old Austin, Texas" and some random modern art photography a fraud of a challenge, demanding you to give it your own meaning. 

At 11:45 am I way to the testing center. A new wave of emotions now irradiated my body now. I felt like an high performance athlete. The spotlight is on me, awaiting my cunning moves. My strategy backed up by the countless repetitions I executed this last 8 months. I was given some simple instructions and shown to the computer were I was to take my exam. 

Time started ticking, 2 hours, 65 questions to test my 8 months of work. I wondered, how so much effort can be distilled into 2 hours. Like a football player taking a penalty. Years of training that are justified by the process of scoring a winning the match. 

I worked through each question, some being obvious only through my arduous study. Others, presented a challenge. This friction between the known questions and the hard ones summoned an internal little voice, familiar to me that said "You should've studied harder." A grueling voice that has been with me, through highs and lows. A dear companion that has tormented me and crowned me with fruitful rewards. Showing up to demand more of me, pushing me to my higher potential. But also, a punisher who flagelattes when I don't reach my objective. 

Since high school I have been managing this thug of war relationship with my internal judge. Its genesis happened during a rebellious phase. Getting close to being expelled from my high school, my family and teachers gave me an ultimatum, correct my act or face the guillotine and get kicked out. I didn't want to leave that school, all of my friends were there and I though that could be the most humiliating thing to happen to me. 

So I had to pull my big boy pants up and shape up. Slowly, my behavior started to change, I started saying 'good morning' to teachers, I focused on my assignments and I began to lay the bricks for a transformation. Though out this process, my internal judge guided me through my decision making, each aimed at improving my reputation with professors. Fast forward to some finessing and diligent persuasion and I graduated with honors from my high school, earning a scholarship for college. The internal judge had saved me.


This conciousness programming has not left me after 15 years. Still pushing me and punishing me for not hustling.
